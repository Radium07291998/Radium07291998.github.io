Web Audio Visualization

12/17/2022

1. Abstract

Audio visualization is the process of obtaining data from audio sources such as frequency, waveform, and other data, converting it into a graphical or visual representation on a screen, and then interacting with it.
As defined, the main process of audio visualization only involves two steps:

  1. Obtaining audio data
  2. Mapping and rendering the data to create a visualization pattern

3. Solution

3.1 Get audio data

Sound has three elements: loudness, pitch, and timbre. Audio visualization mainly focuses on the first two elements, so we are mainly concerned with the two physical attributes corresponding to loudness and pitch, which are amplitude and frequency respectively. Audio is a continuous signal that changes over time, so time is also an important dimension of audio data.

Usually what we refer to as a waveform is a graph with time on the X-axis and amplitude on the Y-axis. The data represented by the waveform can also be called waveform data or time domain data; while the spectrum analysis bar graph (the kind in old-fashioned players) is a graph with frequency on the X-axis and amplitude on the Y-axis, which changes dynamically with audio playback time. This type of graph contains data called frequency data or frequency domain data.

Amplitude, frequency, and time actually form a three-dimensional data space, and time domain and frequency domain are actually results from different perspectives of audio data.
